Programme #UM2030 — International Outlook

Programme for the Rector of the University of Maribor, 2026–2030

This page is an English adaptation of my rectoral programme, prepared for international students, researchers, and partner institutions; it is not a direct translation of the full Slovenian text. The complete programme, written for the academic community of the University of Maribor, is available in Slovenian at program.html and as a PDF document.

A university of the future: connected in knowledge and creativity, courageous in research.

Introduction

The University of Maribor enters the 2026–2030 mandate with a clear ambition: to consolidate its position as a research-intensive, internationally engaged university that places students at the centre of its mission. This document outlines the priorities of my candidacy for Rector, with a particular focus on what they mean for international students, researchers, and partner institutions.

The University of Maribor today is the second-largest university in Slovenia, with seventeen faculties, university library, student dormitories, more than 1,300 researchers, 111 research groups, and 51 research programmes spanning all major scientific and artistic fields. It is a comprehensive university — covering the natural sciences, engineering, medicine, biotechnology, social sciences, humanities, and the arts — and it operates within a network of locations across Slovenia, with faculties in Maribor, Celje, Krško, Brežice, Kranj, and Ljubljana.

In the past four years, as Vice-Rector for Research, I have led the transformation of the University's research system. We have entered the national stable funding scheme, doubled the number of European Research Council applications, increased participation in EU framework programmes, and secured more than 500 million euro for the largest infrastructure investment cycle in the University's history. The next mandate is the time to bring this work to fruition.

1. Students at the Centre

The University exists for its students. This is not a slogan but a starting point: every decision will be assessed by whether it strengthens the student experience, opens pathways into the world, and supports each student in developing their full potential.

In the next mandate, the University will:

  • Build an integrated student support platform that brings together career counselling, psychological support, tutoring, and services for students with special needs and special status — all accessible to every student when they need it.
  • Strengthen the tutoring system with clear standards and training. Mentoring is the foundation of academic success, from the first year through doctoral studies.
  • Expand flexibility and choice within study programmes, giving each student the space to follow their interests, build their strengths, and combine disciplines in ways that prepare them for a complex world.
  • Increase practical experience through expanded student projects with industry partners, study visits to real working environments, and internship placements that give direct insight into potential career paths.
  • Guarantee equal treatment of all students regardless of gender, social background, ethnicity, disability, or any other personal circumstance, and continue strengthening student representation in decision-making at all levels of the University.
  • Engage students in research and the arts from the earliest stages of study, with comprehensive support for student-led research, innovation initiatives, and start-up creation.

2. International Students: A University That Welcomes the World

International students are not an addition to the University — they are part of what makes a contemporary university what it is. The next mandate will build a more systematic, more welcoming, and more thoughtful framework for international students at every stage of their experience at Maribor.

A dedicated digital hub for international students will serve as a single entry point connecting administrative support, career counselling, language resources, accommodation information, and extracurricular activities. Information that international students need will be findable in one place, in clear language, before and during their time at the University.

An expanded portfolio of programmes in English will be developed in dialogue with faculties, with particular attention to graduate-level studies. The recently announced English-language programme in nuclear engineering, developed in partnership with the French Institute for Nuclear Energy (I2EN), is one concrete example of this direction.

Internationalisation at home will ensure that international perspectives, comparative approaches, and global challenges are part of the curriculum for all students — not only those who travel abroad. Intercultural events, joint projects, and shared spaces will build a campus identity that is open to the world.

Integration into the local community: the University commits to making Maribor a place where international students do not only study but also live well — through partnerships with the city, accommodation networks, and student-led initiatives that build genuine community.

3. Mobility and International Partnerships

The University of Maribor sustains a wide network of international links: bilateral agreements with universities across Europe and beyond, participation in European university alliances, Erasmus+ and other mobility frameworks, and joint initiatives within the European Higher Education Area. Together, these partnerships form the institutional framework for a substantial part of our international ambitions — joint study programme development, researcher and student mobility, and shared strategic initiatives.

In the next mandate, international cooperation will not remain at the level of formal membership. We will:

  • Deepen content-rich collaboration with strategic partner universities, with concrete benefits for our students, teachers, and researchers — joint courses, shared modules, and integrated research projects.
  • Expand outbound mobility for students, including short-term study visits, research stays, and artistic residencies abroad. Every student should have the opportunity to encounter another academic environment, another methodological tradition, another cultural context.
  • Streamline credit recognition and administrative procedures for mobility, removing barriers that currently discourage participation.
  • Develop joint and double degree programmes, particularly at the master's and doctoral levels, with strategic international partners.
  • Support inbound mobility with attractive conditions, including English-language course offerings, accommodation support, and integration into university life beyond the classroom.

4. Slovenian Language and International Openness

A university that operates in multiple languages while continuing to preserve, value and protect its cultural heritage demonstrates the kind of intellectual maturity that goes beyond pragmatic adaptation.

International dialogue does not threaten national academic traditions; it sharpens and strengthens them. The University of Maribor will continue to develop Slovenian as a language of scholarship and science, while expanding its capacity to teach, research, and publish in English and other languages. Multilingual capacity is itself a form of critical literacy: a student who can read scholarly literature in several languages, and who understands how the same concept inflects differently across linguistic and cultural contexts, is better prepared for a world that is not governed by a single discourse.

5. Research: Excellence, Programme Cores, and International Partnerships

The University of Maribor is a research-intensive university with rapidly growing international visibility.

Six interdisciplinary programme cores organise our strategic research priorities, integrating researchers across faculties and bringing critical mass to areas of recognised strength:

  • Artificial intelligence and data science in biomedicine
  • Materials and technologies
  • Heritage science and climate change
  • Fundamental research in mathematics and the natural sciences
  • Space research and Earth observation
  • Interdisciplinary research in the social sciences

These programme cores will be expanded and strengthened, with increased funding for early-career researchers, support for major international project applications, and dedicated incentives for European Research Council preparations.

Major research infrastructure investments are underway:

  • RIUM2 — a 25 million euro infrastructure upgrade, including new equipment for the social sciences, humanities, engineering, natural sciences, and medicine.
  • The SAXS beamline at Elettra-Sincrotrone Trieste — a 9 million euro project, jointly with Technical University of Graz, Elettra Sincrotrone Trieste and the University of Primorska, that connects Slovenian researchers to a leading international synchrotron facility.
  • SLAIF (Slovenian AI Factory) — Slovenia's contribution to the European EuroHPC Joint Undertaking, building a next-generation supercomputer with applications optimised for artificial intelligence research.
  • BBMRI — coordinated by the University of Maribor, providing biobanking research infrastructure within the European ESFRI Roadmap.
  • GIGA NMR — a strategic technologies project worth approximately 27.5 million euro, in partnership with the National Institute of Chemistry.

International recognition through participation in key initiatives: the Barcelona Declaration on Open Research Information, the Coalition for Advancing Research Assessment (CoARA), the EOSC Association, and the European Citizen Science Association (ECSA). The University holds the European HRS4R quality mark for human resources strategy in research.

The European Research Council is a strategic priority. ERC application numbers have doubled in the past four years, and the next mandate will introduce two-year preparatory grants for high-quality ERC project applications.

6. Doctoral Studies as an Institutional Commitment

Doctoral studies are not the private project of supervisor and candidate. They are an institutional commitment of the university to the young researchers who will carry science forward in the coming decades.

In the next mandate, the University will:

  • Renew the doctoral studies regulation to reflect the needs of programmes across all faculties and contemporary international standards.
  • Expand co-tutelle and joint degree arrangements with strategic international partners.
  • Strengthen support services for doctoral candidates, including monitoring of progress, transparent supervision standards, and protection against the isolation and burnout that can undermine doctoral work.
  • Establish clear and sustainable criteria for supervisors and course leaders, integrating active research engagement, international embeddedness, and pedagogical preparation — while respecting the diversity of disciplines and research traditions.

7. Artificial Intelligence and Critical Digital Literacy

Artificial intelligence is reshaping how we study, research, and create. The University is the place where we learn to assess this transformation critically — not merely as users of new tools, but as a community that maintains the capacity for independent thought.

Critical digital literacy is not a side-topic. It is at the core of the contemporary university's mission, in an age when algorithmic authority increasingly shapes access to knowledge. The University will develop institutional guidelines for the responsible use of AI in teaching and research, while keeping the cultivation of human judgement at the centre of education.

Thinking remains our responsibility.

8. Open Science

The University of Maribor has built a comprehensive open science framework, including the University's Open Science Policy, the Digital Library, and a network of Open Science Ambassadors. The University is signatory to the Barcelona Declaration on Open Research Information and a member of CoARA, the EOSC Association, and ECSA.

In the next mandate, we will continue to develop open science as the way the University returns to society what society entrusts to it. Open access to research outputs, open educational resources, and the FAIR principles for data stewardship will remain institutional priorities.

9. The Arts as a Field of Research

Artistic creation at the University of Maribor is excellent and richly diverse. Art is not decoration alongside science; it is an equivalent field of inquiry into the world.

The next mandate will:

  • Establish a dedicated arts research centre, providing institutional standing for the integration of artistic and scientific knowledge.
  • Develop educational content leading to academic education in the arts, with the prospect of establishing a new faculty member dedicated to artistic studies.
  • Support artistic research project applications to Horizon Europe, Creative Europe, and the New European Bauhaus, with dedicated incentives within the development pillar of research funding.
  • Expand FestUM (the Festival of Science and Art) and the UMetnost exhibition cycle as platforms that bring together researchers, artists, students, and the wider public.

10. Infrastructure for the Future

The University of Maribor is undertaking the largest investment cycle in its history, with more than 500 million euro already secured. This includes:

  • New buildings for the Faculty of Health Sciences, the Medical Faculty (Hofer Triangle), the three faculties on Koroška Street (Pedagogical Faculty, Faculty of Arts, Faculty of Natural Sciences and Mathematics), the Faculty of Mechanical Engineering, the Faculty of Chemistry and Chemical Engineering, and others.
  • Reconstruction and expansion of the Faculty of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science, the Faculty of Civil Engineering, Transportation Engineering and Architecture, the Faculty of Logistics, the Faculty of Energy Technology, and the Faculty of Criminal Justice and Security.
  • Student housing: the renovated Student Hall 15 has just been delivered; further renovations are planned.

These investments are not only buildings. They are modern teaching and research environments designed for the next generations of students, researchers, and partners.

11. Autonomy as a Constitutional Value

University autonomy is not a privilege requested from ministries. It is a constitutional value, defined in Article 58 of the Slovenian Constitution and operationalised in the higher education and research laws. It is what gives the University the freedom to research, to create art, and to teach according to its own academic judgement.

I will defend this autonomy in dialogue with the state, in relation to market pressures, and in relation to all who would treat the University as an instrument of external interests. Autonomy is sustained by the excellence of our work — research, teaching, and the daily efforts of academic staff, professional services, and students. It is sustained by community, not by individual networks.

12. Leadership Through Trust

The University is a community. It is led not from above but together — as a community aware that no decision is purely technical, and that no leadership is purely managerial. Leadership in conditions of academic complexity demands trust, collaboration, clearly defined responsibilities, and the courage to acknowledge what we do not yet know.

This is the disposition I bring to the rectorship. Professional services, teachers, researchers, artists, and students are not implementers of decisions made elsewhere. They are the community that actually creates the University.

A Continuous Path Forward

The change the University of Maribor needs is not a rupture with its past. It is the continuation of the path we have already started — with quiet determination, a clear goal, and respect for the work that has been done. The achievements of the past mandate — entry into stable research funding, doubled ERC applications, the launch of programme cores, the development of open science, and the unprecedented investment cycle — are not behind us. They are the foundation on which we build forward, together.

Ambitious, yet grounded. #UM2030

Dean Korošak
