The Membrane Mean-Field Model

A single urban microlocation under the collective membrane
Mean-field reduction of the Axelrod–membrane network model to a single node coupled to an effective environment. The neighbourhood is replaced by a homogeneous bath characterised by mean trait values; the stochastic interaction, climate forcing, cooling propagation, and noise yield a deterministic dynamical system for the continuous trait vector σ(t) ∈ [0,1]⁷ of a single microlocation.
▶

Mean-Field Derivation

In the full network model, node i interacts stochastically with neighbour j chosen at random. The mean-field approximation replaces the heterogeneous neighbourhood with a single effective environment characterised by the mean trait vector σ̄ = (σ̄¹, …, σ̄⁷) and an effective coupling strength w̄.

The expected change in trait f per time step has four contributions: Axelrod interaction with the bath, climate forcing, cooling propagation from the effective green/blue neighbourhood fraction g, and noise.

dσᶠ/dt = J_axelrod + J_climate + J_cool + J_noise

Axelrod flux. The interaction probability with the environment is the weighted similarity S = (1/7)·Σ (1 − |σᶠ − σ̄ᶠ|). When interaction occurs, the trait is pulled toward the environment mean. The expected flux is:

J_axelrod = w̄ · S · (σ̄ᶠ − σᶠ) · (1 − δ_{f,H} · ρ_H)

where w̄ is the effective coupling, S the mean-field similarity, and the last factor enforces heritage rigidity on the heritage feature.

Climate forcing. Each physical feature degrades according to the temperature-dependent forcing function ϕf(T):

J_climate = −ϕᶠ(T(t)) · σᶠ · (1 − ρ_type)

where ρtype is the resilience (ρG for green, 0.8 for blue, 0 for built nodes). The forcing functions are: thermal mass degrades above 20°C, vegetation above 25°C (severely above 35°C), albedo proportional to anomaly, ET above 28°C, water above 30°C.

Cooling propagation. If the node is a built element, the effective green/blue neighbourhood fraction g drives trait improvement in vegetation and evapotranspiration:

J_cool = p_cool · g · w̄ · (σ̄ᶠ_green − σᶠ) for f ∈ {vegetation, ET}, σ̄ᶠ_green > σᶠ

Noise. The mean effect of uniform noise on trait f is a restoring force toward the midpoint:

J_noise = η · (0.5 − σᶠ)

The full mean-field ODE system (7 equations for physical features) is integrated numerically with an Euler scheme at monthly time steps. The heritage constraint feature σ⁸ evolves only through the Axelrod channel, blocked with probability ρH.

Order parameters. The local GLI is the mean of all 7 normalised physical traits. The TAI measures the Euclidean distance from the initial state. The effective temperature T(t) = T₀ + Ṫ·(t/12) + ΔT·sin(2πt/12) drives the seasonal and secular forcing.

Steady-state analysis. Setting dσᶠ/dt = 0 yields the fixed point at which Axelrod attraction to the bath, climate degradation, cooling, and noise balance. The critical warming rate Ṫ* at which the fixed-point GLI drops below a threshold defines the microlocation's climate resilience. This threshold depends on the node type, heritage rigidity, green fraction, and coupling strength — all tunable below.
